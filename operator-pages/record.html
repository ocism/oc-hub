Profile

This operator showed up one day at the office as Record Scratch, requesting to join and be hired. Despite his lack of previous experience, his determination to learn how to fight has landed him a spot in the reserve operators’ list. He displayed an unfaltering willingness to adapt to a new lifestyle as an operator for Rhodes Island. An investigation shows potential in his physical and mental capabilities, proving him worthy of joining our cause as a soldier and possible test subject.

Archive File 2

Increase Trust to 100.

It's quite strange. Record was reported to have a very strict regiment; 06:00 to wake, and 22:00 to bed. His routine is marked by the clock, from training to eating to working. Anyone could see that he did everything to the best of his ability, even if it was difficult to see his eyes through his sunglasses.

That's not the strange part. The really weird part is how in TERRA does someone like him keep such a rigid schedule!? Operators have seen him sponge-skate down the hallway, switch out sugar for salt, and replace perfectly good pie filling with toothpaste. There were also numerous reports about Operator Record enabling dangerous behavior such as burning down another operators' headquarters. When questioned, he only shrugged and said he helped calculate the medical bills and damage costs if they really went forward with their idea, and that he had no other part in carrying out the plan. Ridiculous! Such lack of self restraint seems so uncharacteristic of him!

Archive File 3

Increase Trust to 150.

Record Scratch. Noun. The sound of a phonograph needle scraping against a record, causing an interruption and calling immediate attention.

Record Scratch. Proper noun. An operator who has mastered the art of interrupting people who pry about his past by calling immediate attention to the present.

We found an old pamphlet with an image of a young musician holding a violin. The pages were practically falling apart, but the "Allen Bach" in large font was unmistakable. At first, we thought it was one of his relatives, or a sibling perhaps, because he had never talked about his family before. Lots of performances showed up when we looked up the name. He had nicknames such as "rising star" and "natural talent", but the most bizarre news that showed up said "Disappearance of Allen Bach." His last performance scheduled was never heard. Apparently, he didn't even show up. We would have read more if we weren't suddenly jump scared by Record's sudden appearance in our office.

'I was looking for papers for Finn, but I see that you found some private information.'

We weren't sure if he was upset (it's hard to tell through those sunglasses!), so we tried apologizing, but he waved it off.

'That's in the past now. Allen Bach doesn't play anymore. Ah, you're so tense! Relax a bit! Life's too short to get hung up on something like this!'

We were stunned silent as he beatboxed on his way out.
